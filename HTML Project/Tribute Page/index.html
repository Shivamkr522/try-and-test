Steves Jobs

An American entrepreneur, industrial designer, business magnate, media proprietor, and investor

He was the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple; the chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar; a member of The Walt Disney Company's board of directors following its acquisition of Pixar; and the founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT.

Here's a time line of Steve Job's life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California.
  • 1968 - Went to Homestead High School
  • 1972 - Went to Reed College. After a year he dropped from there
  • 1974 - Back in Silicon Valley in the autumn of 1974, Jobs reconnected with Stephen Wozniak, a former high school friend who was working for the Hewlett-Packard Company.
  • 1976 - Launched Apple I called as logic board. Then later on remade it as Apple II, complete with keyboard.
  • 1977 - Piblicize Apple II after its completion.
  • 1983 - Made it's entry in Fortune Fifty and recriuted John Sculley as CEO.
  • 1986 - Acquired Pixar, made a film Toy Story finished in 1995. but eventually sold it to Disney in 2006.
  • 1996 - In late 1996 Apple, saddled by huge financial losses and on the verge of collapse, but Steve Jobs had saved his company, and in the process reestablished himself as a master high-technology marketer and visionary.
  • 2001 - In 2001 Jobs started reinventing Apple for the 21st century. That was the year that Apple introduced iTunes, a computer program for playing music and for converting music to the compact MP3 digital format commonly used in computers and other digital devices.
  • 2007 - Jobs officially changed the name of the company to Apple Inc. Jobs took the company into the telecommunications business with the introduction of the touch-screen iPhone, a mobile telephone with capabilities for playing MP3s and videos and for accessing the Internet.
  • 2003 - Jobs was diagnosed with a rare form of pancreatic cancer. He put off surgery for about nine months while he tried alternative medicine approaches
  • 2008 - Jobs lost significant weight, which produced considerable speculation that his cancer was back.
  • 2009 - the Wall Street Journal reported that Jobs had received a liver transplant the previous April.
  • 2011 - Jobs took another medical leave of absence. In August he resigned as CEO but became chairman. He died two months later.
